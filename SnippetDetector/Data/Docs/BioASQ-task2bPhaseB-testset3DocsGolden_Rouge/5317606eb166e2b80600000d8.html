GRK2 and HDAC6 colocalize in the lamellipodia of migrating cells, leading to local tubulin deacetylation and enhanced motility. GRK2, which levels inversely correlate with the extent of - tubulin acetylation in epithelial cells and fibroblasts, directly associates with and phosphorylates HDAC6 to stimulate - tubulin deacetylase activity. We identify G protein-coupled receptor kinase 2 as a key novel stimulator of HDAC6. The distribution of acetylated alpha-tubulin is tightly controlled and stereotypic. Reversible acetylation of alpha-tubulin has been implicated in regulating microtubule stability and function. TPPP/p25 is an unstructured protein that induces microtubule polymerization in vitro and is aligned along the microtubule network in transfected mammalian cells. Our results highlight the role of alpha-tubulin acetylation in mediating the localization of microtubule-associated proteins. GRK2-HDAC6-mediated regulation of tubulin acetylation also modulates cellular spreading. This GRK2-HDAC6 functional interaction may have important implications in pathological contexts related to epithelial cell migration. HDAC6 is a major cytoplasmic a-tubulin deacetylase that is involved in cell motility and adhesion. Consistent with the hypothesis that tubulin deacetylase activity might affect cell response to the anti-motility activity of taxanes, we found that overexpression of the tubulin deacetylase SIRT2 increased cell motility and reduced cell response to the anti-motility activity of paclitaxel. .
