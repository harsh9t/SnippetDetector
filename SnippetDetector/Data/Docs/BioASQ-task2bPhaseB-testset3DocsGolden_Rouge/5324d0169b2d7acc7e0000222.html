The only FDA-approved dermatological indication of finasteride is androgenetic alopecia. Finasteride (Propecia) was approved by the FDA in 1998 for treating men with androgenetic alopecia. In addition, low-level light therapy (LLLT) has recently been approved by the United States Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for the treatment of hair loss. Topical monoxidil and oral finasteride are commonly in use and have FDA approval for the treatment of male androgenetic alopecia; dutasteride, a type I and II 5-alpha-reductase inhibitor, is on hold in Phase III trials.
