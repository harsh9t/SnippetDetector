Yellow fever virus is transmitted by mosquitoes and is restricted to Africa, Central and South America and the Caribbean. Yellow fever virus is a flavivirus, and there is only one antigenic type. It was taken to the Americas by the early slave traders, and nowadays reported in Africa, America, Asia and Europe.
