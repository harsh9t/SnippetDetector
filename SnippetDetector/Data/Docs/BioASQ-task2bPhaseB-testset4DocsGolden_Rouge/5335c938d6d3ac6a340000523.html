Caring for incontinent patients in the long-term care setting was shown to result in substantial additional costs, Overactive bladder (OAB) is a highly prevalent condition among older patients, and its presence is associated with the use of substantial healthcare resources and economic costs. The 1995 societal cost of incontinence for individuals aged 65 years and older was $26. 3 billion, or $3565 per individual with urinary incontinence. For individuals 65 years of age and older these costs are substantial, increasing from $8. 2 billion (1984 dollars) to $16. 4 billion (1993 dollars). .
