Since the landmark study on rheumatoid arthritis, many reports have suggested that physiological changes during pregnancy often induce remission of systemic and cutaneous inflammatory diseases. (PMID: 12839599) We found an inverse correlation between the number of pregnancies and age at onset of RA (PMID: 22012422) an early age at first birth increased the odds, and the postpartum period was confirmed as high risk for RA onset. The protective effect of repeated exposure to the ameliorating hormonal and immunological changes of pregnancy may counterbalance the effect of early exposure to the postpartum reversal of these changes. (PMID: 23070994) A larger number of pregnancies and late menopause show a protective effect, delaying the onset of the disease. (PMID: 22012422).
