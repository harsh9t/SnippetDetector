Pfeiffer syndrome is divided into three clinical subtypes (PMID: 16740155) Type 1 "classic" Pfeiffer syndrome involves individuals with mild manifestations including brachycephaly, midface hypoplasia and finger and toe abnormalities; it is associated with normal intelligence and generally good outcome. Type 2 consists of cloverleaf skull, extreme proptosis, finger and toe abnormalities, elbow ankylosis or synostosis, developmental delay and neurological complications. Type 3 is similar to type 2 but without a cloverleaf skull. (PMID: 16740155) Type 1 represents the less severe cases, while types 2 and 3 are the more severe cases.
