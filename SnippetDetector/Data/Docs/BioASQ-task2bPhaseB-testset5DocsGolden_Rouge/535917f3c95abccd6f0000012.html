grobacterium tumefaciens infects plants and induces the formation of tumors called crown galls by integrating the transferred-DNA (T-DNA) region of the Ti-plasmid into the plant nuclear genome grobacterium tumefaciens induces crown gall tumors on plants by transferring a nucleoprotein complex, the T-complex, from the bacterium to the plant cell gall tumors develop after integration of the T-DNA of virulent Agrobacterium tumefaciens strains into the plant genome Agrobacterium tumefaciens causes crown gall disease in a wide range of plant species. The neoplastic growth at the infection sites is caused by transferring, integrating, and expressing transfer DNA (T-DNA) from A. tumefaciens into plant cells.
